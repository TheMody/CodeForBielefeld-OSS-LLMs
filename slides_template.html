What “open” actually means

“Open source LLM” usually means open weights

Open weights
You get the trained parameters — download, run, fine-tune. This is what “open” means for almost every model.
the norm
Open data
The training data is published. Very few models (e.g. OLMo-style research projects).
rare
Open training code
The full recipe to reproduce the model from scratch.
rare
Licenses still differ: Apache 2.0 (Qwen — do anything) · Gemma license · Llama license (usage terms attached)
WHY CARE?
  • Privacy — DSGVO!Citizen data never leaves your machine
  • Cost & offlineNo per-token bill, works without internet
  • No vendor lock-inThe model file is yours, forever
  • Fine-tunableAdapt it to your domain (→ LoRA, later)
A very good fit for civic tech and public administration.

Quantization

JPEG for neural networks

Weights are just numbers. Quantization stores them with fewer bits — lossy, like JPEG, but you barely notice.

Inference vs. training

You can run it. You cannot train it.

MEMORY FOR A 4B MODEL
Inference Q4
2.5 GB — easy
Inference FP16
8 GB — tight
Full training
50+ GB — no
weights 8 gradients 8 optimizer 16 activations 18+
Training a 27B: ~350+ GB → multi-GPU cluster.
Inference is reading the book.
Training is writing it.
WHY THE GAP?
10–20× the memory of Q4 inference
backprop keeps gradients + Adam states + activations alongside the weights
Training can’t be 4-bit
tiny gradient updates vanish in coarse number formats — precision is required
And it’s compute, too
Gemma-class pretraining: thousands of GPUs for weeks — this laptop: centuries
11
The core math slide. Training needs weights + gradients + Adam optimizer states + activations ≈ 10–20× the memory of quantized inference — and it can’t be aggressively quantized because tiny gradient updates vanish in 4-bit. Compute side: Gemma-class pretraining = thousands of GPUs × weeks. TODO: fun back-of-envelope — “training Gemma 4 on this laptop ≈ X years” (FLOPs of pretraining ÷ laptop FLOPs).
